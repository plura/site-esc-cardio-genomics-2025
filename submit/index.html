Abstract Information

A maximum of 200 characters typed in lower-case letters, except for abbreviations and study names. Please be careful that your title might be truncated if you copy and paste it into the field. Do not include authors, institutions, city names, trademarks and abbreviations in the title.

All sources of financial support (including government grants) for this research must be filed under this heading and not in the abstract text. All grant funding agency abbreviations should be spelt out. This information will be published in the abstract itself. The submitter is asked to indicate the type of funding source(s) and name it/them, if any. This information may be used in the context of ESC research projects.

Abstract

Abstracts must be in English (UK spelling), with correct grammar. Have it reviewed if unsure. Recommended structure:

  • Background
  • Purpose
  • Methods
  • Results
  • Conclusions

Max size: 35 lines or 3,000 characters (excluding title and authors).

Do not include any identifying details like:

  • Author or institute names
  • Cities
  • Trademarks or company names
  • Websites or emails

Non-compliant abstracts may be rejected or edited.

Use this field for references or citations. Do not include them in the main text.

Limit acronyms and abbreviations. Define them on first use in parentheses. Avoid copyright and trademark symbols.

Research with human subjects must follow the Declaration of Helsinki. Informed consent must be available if applicable. Avoid using identifiable patient photos.

If your abstract is selected, it may be published in the European Journal of Preventive Cardiology (EJPC). Please indicate your consent for this potential publication.

Abstract Submission Guidelines & Data Privacy Summary

By confirming you've read the submission rules, you also confirm you have your co-authors' permission to share their data with the ESC. The data collected is used to create the ESC Cardio Genomics abstract programme and to list all contributing authors. Only listed authors will be acknowledged if the abstract is accepted.
Data is handled by ESC staff, committees, and volunteers, and may be shared with contractors and related scientific organisations for the Congress. It will be stored for 20 years.
Under EU Regulation 2016/679, you have the right to access, correct, delete, or restrict the use of your data, or object to its processing. These rights can be exercised by withdrawing the abstract per ESC’s terms.
